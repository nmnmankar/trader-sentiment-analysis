## Analysis Summary

### Methodology

The project analyzes the relationship between **market sentiment (Fear vs Greed)** and **trader behavior and performance** using two datasets: a Bitcoin Fear & Greed sentiment dataset and historical trader transaction data from Hyperliquid.

First, both datasets were cleaned by checking for missing values, removing duplicates, and converting timestamps into a consistent daily date format. The trader dataset was then merged with the sentiment dataset using the date field so that each trade could be associated with the corresponding market sentiment.

Next, several key metrics were created to analyze trader behavior and performance, including:

* **Closed PnL** to measure trader profitability
* **Trade frequency** to analyze how active traders are during different sentiment conditions
* **Average trade size (USD)** to observe risk-taking behavior
* **Win rate** to estimate the proportion of profitable trades

Exploratory data analysis and visualizations were used to compare these metrics between **Fear** and **Greed** market conditions. Additionally, traders were segmented into groups such as **frequent vs infrequent traders** to identify behavioral patterns.

---

### Key Insights

1. **Trader behavior changes with market sentiment.**
   During Greed periods, traders tend to place larger trades and show higher trading activity, indicating increased risk appetite.

2. **Market Fear periods show more cautious behavior.**
   Traders reduce trade sizes and activity during Fear conditions, suggesting risk aversion and uncertainty.

3. **Frequent traders generally achieve higher cumulative profitability.**
   More active traders tend to generate better overall results compared to infrequent traders, possibly due to experience or better market timing.

---

### Strategy Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following trading strategies can be suggested:

**1. Risk Reduction During Fear Periods**
When the market sentiment indicates Fear, traders should consider reducing position sizes and avoiding high-risk trades. This approach helps protect capital during uncertain market conditions.

**2. Controlled Aggression During Greed Periods**
When market sentiment is Greed, traders may increase trading activity and position sizes moderately to take advantage of market momentum, while still maintaining risk management rules.

These strategies highlight how sentiment-driven insights can help traders make **more informed and disciplined trading decisions**.
